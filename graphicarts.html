Project Objective

Because of the confusing architecture of the graphic arts building at the university I attended, when people walk into an entrance they aren’t sure if they are on the first or second floor. The original maps had a confusing layout and were outdated. I redesigned the building maps to provide a clearer understanding of the building layout and reflect the organizations listed in the building and their location. I also designed additional signs to be hung from certain entrances to provide additional guidance on where to go.

Problems

Where am I?
Because of the confusing architecture of the building, when people walk into an entrance they aren’t sure if they are on the first or second floor. It should be more obvious than a map. There are four entrances to the building: two of them takes the individual to the first floor and two of them takes the individual to the second floor depending on which side of the building the individual is on.

Outdated Maps
Maps are outdated and don’t have a current list of all the organizations located in the building.

Lack of noticeability
There are an excessive number of posters on the wall that can lead to people barely scanning the wall and not noticing important posters. Less is more. Various students I spoke to that enter the Graphic Arts building often aren’t aware of the third floor.

Lack of Consistency
The maps throughout the building changed orientations. Some didn’t include all the three floors.

Competitive Analysis

I performed a competitive analysis to see what made other maps effective in their design.

Example 1

  • Minimal Design
  • Stacked floors on top of each other
  • Allows the user to quickly visualize the structure of the building and where the elevators/stairs are located on each part of the building

Example 2

  • Strong contrasting colors
  • Dark background makes colors stand out more
  • Rather than labeling rooms themselves, uses lines to point to each section

Example 3

  • Smaller color palette
  • List of entities at the bottom of the map

Solution

Where am I?
The maps aren’t enough an indicator for individuals to know which floor they’re on when they first walk in. We can add additional hanging signage, similar to the ones on the second floor, inside each entrance with text “2nd floor” or “1st floor”.

Outdated Maps
Create an excel document of all organizations in Building 26 to display on the map that include the organization name, contact, email, building floor, and status.

Use logo entities to showcase various organizations in building 26.

Lack of noticeability
Current solution: Placement of maps chosen based on most available visual real estate.

Forward-looking solution: Consider reducing the number of posters on the walls of the second floor so important content will stand out more among the wall.

Lack of consistency
Each of the maps will display all three floors in the same design and color.
“You are here” will change depending on where the map is.

Map Design

We were able to print and mount the signs onto the new locations. Four of these now hang in each of the entrances of my university’s graphic arts building.
